# Trading Log: 2026-04-23

## Market Overview & Events
- **Geopolitical Shock (Noon CT)**: Major volatility spike following reports from Iranian media that the civilian government officially lost its "seat at the grill" to the IRGC (Revolutionary Guard) immediately preceding treaty talks with the US.
- **Two-Tier Sell-off**: The market experienced a sharp, two-staged liquidation. The first wave was followed by a brief 5-minute consolidation before a second, more aggressive leg down to the daily lows.
- **Late Session Recovery**: After bottoming out, the market staged a massive reversal, returning nearly to pre-event price levels by the close.

## System Performance & Execution
- **Order Fragmentation & Dependency**: Due to the suddenness of the move, the application broke larger orders into smaller increments to avoid flipping existing positions. This created a high degree of fill dependency and contributed to the largest live-sim mismatch to date.
- **Drawdown**: Total loss for the day was approximately **$5,000** on a **$35,000** portfolio—a significant and unhealthy drawdown (~14%).

## Manual Interventions
- **Failed Reversion Play**: Intervened during the first wave of selling with the expectation of a quick mean reversion. Instead, the market consolidated and then broke lower. I held the position through the "heat" until near the daily low.
- **Panic Exit & "Giving In"**: Closed out the defensive/intervention positions at the worst possible time—right before the massive late-afternoon rally.
- **Speculative Put Buying (01:00 PM)**: Entered a discretionary long put position (single leg, 20 delta) speculating on a "regime change" event and a shift to a "long-term war" narrative. This speculation failed to materialize and resulted in an additional **$400** loss in live PnL while attempting to chase the live-sim mismatch.

## Lessons Learned & Future Adjustments
- **Intervention Objective**: Manual intervention during sudden moves or position flips is necessary to synchronize live and simulated states, but it must be strictly non-speculative. The sole purpose should be reconciliation, not profit-seeking based on market "guesses."
- **Hedging Dependency**: In cases where trades have fill dependencies, use temporary single-leg hedges to manage delta rather than discretionary directional bets.
- **Discipline & Trust**: Speculation kills the edge. Trust the established process and maintain mechanical discipline, even during high-stress geopolitical events.
- **Risk De-leveraging**: Reduced trading size from **5 units to 3 units** (via `portfolio_interval_minutes` adjustment) to protect capital until the portfolio reaches new highs and stabilizes.
